That afternoon, when the wind had died down and the barnyard was
quiet
and warm, the grey goose led her seven goslings off the nest and out
into the world. Mr. Zuckerman spied them when he came with Wilbur's
supper.
"Well, hello there!" he said, smiling all over. "Let's see ... one,

CHAPTER 7
Bad News
Wilbur liked Charlotte better and better each day. Her campaign
against
insects seemed sensible and useful. Hardly anybody around the farm
had
a good word to say for a fly. Flies spent their time pestering others.
The cows hated them. The horses detested them. The sheep loathed
them.
Mr. and Mrs. Zuckerman were always complaining about them, and
putting
up screens.
Wilbur admired the way Charlotte managed. He was particularly glad
that
she always put her victim to sleep before eating it.
